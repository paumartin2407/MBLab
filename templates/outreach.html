Outreach

    Lola Martin-Bermudo is one of the founders of the DrosAfrica Trust. The organisation aims to build an African biomedical research community using Drosophila melanogaster as a model system.


    Follow @DrosAfrica

    The impact that research has on shaping the future of societies is perhaps as significant as never before. However, African researchers produce only around one percent of the world's research. The DrosAfrica project was founded to contribute to changing this by training and establishing a connected community of researchers using Drosophila as a model system to investigate biomedical problems in Africa. Since founding, the project has trained eighty scientists from numerous African countries, and continues to grow. We believe DrosAfrica has lighted the fuse for the use of Drosophila by African researchers as a model system to help scientists to pursue competitive and groundbreaking projects.
